Quantitative Methodology

The Fama-French model showed PARA had significant exposure to value and profitability factors‚Äîsuggesting mean reversion potential from depressed multiples. Price action from August 2023 to August 2024 showed a 27% drawdown from $15.09 to $11.00, creating an attractive entry point. When I compared it against industry peers, PARA was trading at a discount despite owning comparable content assets and streaming infrastructure.

Technical Capabilities

Built Excel models for pricing bonds, calculating yields, and measuring interest rate risk. The framework handles corporate and government bonds under different rate scenarios.

Core Modeling Components
  • Present Value: Time-value calculations for bonds with semi-annual coupons across different maturities
  • Yield-to-Maturity: Iterative solving to back out implied yields from market prices
  • Duration & Modified Duration: Measures of interest rate sensitivity for portfolio immunization
  • Convexity: Second-order price sensitivity‚Äîcritical for large rate moves
  • Scenario Analysis: Testing parallel and non-parallel yield curve shifts

Methodology

The model uses the standard bond pricing equation with five variables: periods to maturity (N), discount rate (I/Y), present value (PV), periodic coupon payment (PMT), and face value (FV). Default setup assumes semi-annual coupons and $1,000 par value, consistent with US Treasuries and investment-grade corporates.

Example: A bond with 10 semi-annual periods, 6% annual discount rate (3% per period), $50 semi-annual coupon, and $1,000 face value shows premium/discount pricing dynamics. The model also handles effective annual rate (EAR) conversions and builds time-series schedules across the bond's life.

Practical Applications
  • Duration matching for asset-liability management
  • Relative value through yield spread analysis
  • Interest rate hedging using duration and convexity
  • Bond ladder construction for predictable cash flows
  • Credit spread decomposition‚Äîseparating rate risk from default risk

Technical Proficiency

The Excel build uses nested financial functions (PV, FV, RATE, PMT), array formulas for time-series, and goal seek for backing out implied yields. Date arithmetic handles settlement/maturity conventions, day-count methods (30/360, Actual/Actual), and accrued interest.
