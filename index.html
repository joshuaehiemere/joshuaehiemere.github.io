Technical Capabilities

Developed comprehensive Excel-based bond valuation models demonstrating mastery of fixed income pricing mechanics, duration analysis, and convexity calculations. The framework enables precise valuation of corporate and government bonds under varying interest rate scenarios.

Core Modeling Components
  • Present Value Calculation: Built time-value-of-money models using periodic discount rates to price bonds with semi-annual coupon payments against varying maturity structures
  • Yield-to-Maturity (YTM): Implemented iterative solving algorithms to calculate implied yields from market prices, enabling relative value analysis across bond universe
  • Duration & Modified Duration: Calculated Macaulay duration and modified duration metrics to quantify interest rate sensitivity and portfolio immunization strategies
  • Convexity Analysis: Modeled second-order price sensitivity to capture non-linear bond price responses to large interest rate movements
  • Scenario Analysis: Constructed parallel and non-parallel yield curve shift scenarios to stress-test portfolio valuations

Methodology

The valuation framework utilizes the fundamental bond pricing equation incorporating five key variables: periods to maturity (N), market discount rate (I/Y), present value (PV), periodic coupon payment (PMT), and face value (FV). Default assumptions include semi-annual coupon frequency and $1,000 par value, consistent with US Treasury and investment-grade corporate bond conventions.

Example application: A bond with 10 semi-annual periods, 6% annual discount rate (3% per period), $50 semi-annual coupon, and $1,000 face value demonstrates premium/discount pricing dynamics relative to par. The model calculates effective annual rate (EAR) conversions and creates time-series valuation schedules across the bond's life.

Practical Applications

  • Interest rate risk hedging using duration and convexity metrics
  • Bond ladder construction for predictable cash flow generation
  • Credit spread decomposition separating interest rate and default risk components

Technical Proficiency

The Excel implementation demonstrates advanced formula construction including nested financial functions (PV, FV, RATE, PMT), array formulas for time-series calculations, and goal seek functionality for implied yield calculations. Date arithmetic handles settlement/maturity conventions, day-count adjustments (30/360, Actual/Actual), and accrued interest calculations.

Technical Overview

Implemented Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity (GARCH) model to forecast time-varying volatility in equity returns. The GARCH(1,1) specification captures volatility clustering and mean reversion properties observed in financial time series, providing superior risk forecasts compared to constant volatility assumptions.

Model Specification & Parameters
  • GARCH(1,1) Equation: σ²ₜ = ω + α·ε²ₜ₋₁ + β·σ²ₜ₋₁, where σ²ₜ represents conditional variance at time t
  • Estimated Parameters: α (ARCH term) = 0.0705, β (GARCH term) = 0.8759, indicating high volatility persistence
  • Long-run Variance (VL): 0.000211, representing unconditional variance level
  • Persistence (α + β): 0.9464, demonstrating strong mean-reversion in volatility with half-life of approximately 12 trading days
  • Maximum Likelihood Estimation: Total in-sample log-likelihood of 3,752.92, optimized using precision threshold of 0.01

Analytical Framework

The implementation utilized maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) to calibrate model parameters on historical return data from August 2012 to August 2016 (in-sample period). The GARCH specification models conditional variance as function of previous period's squared residual (ARCH component, α) and previous period's conditional variance (GARCH component, β).

Historical in-sample mean return of 0.0085% demonstrates slight positive drift, while the high persistence parameter (α + β = 0.946) indicates volatility shocks decay slowly, requiring approximately 19 days for 50% mean reversion. This characteristic is consistent with equity market behavior where volatility clustering persists across multiple trading sessions.

Backtesting & Validation

Out-of-sample backtesting from August 2016 to December 2021 (1,492 observations) assessed model predictive accuracy using confidence interval coverage tests. The model achieved 69.3% accuracy at 1-standard-deviation confidence level (68.3% theoretical coverage), demonstrating excellent calibration. This validates the GARCH framework's ability to generate reliable one-step-ahead volatility forecasts for risk management applications.

Quantitative Applications
  • Value-at-Risk (VaR): Dynamic VaR calculation using time-varying volatility forecasts for portfolio risk limits
  • Option Pricing: Conditional volatility inputs for Black-Scholes and local volatility models
  • Portfolio Optimization: Time-varying covariance matrix estimation for mean-variance optimization
  • Risk-Adjusted Performance: Sharpe ratio calculation with realized volatility from GARCH forecasts
  • Volatility Trading: Statistical arbitrage opportunities in variance swaps and VIX derivatives

Technical Implementation

The Excel model features recursive variance calculations with circular reference handling, implementing iterative solver routines to maximize log-likelihood function. Daily returns are calculated from adjusted closing prices, with residuals defined as demeaned returns. The backtesting framework compares realized returns against predicted confidence intervals, tracking hit rates across different standard deviation thresholds (1σ, 2σ, 3σ).

Rolling standard deviation comparison (30-day window) demonstrates GARCH model's superior performance in capturing volatility regime shifts compared to simple historical volatility measures. The graphical output visualizes conditional variance evolution alongside actual return volatility, highlighting model's ability to anticipate volatility spikes during market stress periods.
